**⭐ PART 1 — THE CTA PROBLEM**

**Why CTAs Were Breaking the Website**

**1. CTAs were placed too early**

Identity must be activated **before** direction is given. If a CTA appears before resonance, the reader feels pushed.

**2. CTAs were mismatched to the page’s emotional temperature**

Every page has a temperature:

  - Cold

  - Warm

  - Engaged

  - Activated

CTAs must match the temperature. If the CTA energy is wrong, the reader disconnects.

**3. CTAs were not aligned with the House architecture**

Your House is geometric. Your CTAs must follow the geometry.

**4. CTAs were not treated as identity thresholds**

A CTA is not a “next step.” It is a **transition in identity**.

**5. CTAs were not consistent across pages**

Inconsistency breaks sovereignty. Sovereignty requires structure.

**6. CTAs were not placed at the correct narrative moment**

Every page has a narrative arc:

  - Strike

  - Resonance

  - Identity

  - Clarity

  - Threshold

CTAs must appear at the **threshold**, not before.

**⭐ PART 4 — THE CTA TYPES**

**The Four CTA Energies (Your VA Must Memorize These)**

**1. The Invitation (Cold)**

Soft. Spacious. Identity‑aligned. Used when the reader is in reflection.

Examples:

  - “If this is speaking to you…”

  - “If this feels like your season…”

**2. The Directive (Warm)**

Clear. Grounded. Decisive. Used when the reader is already activated.

Examples:

  - “Start here.”

  - “Begin the Return.”

**3. The Ascension (Engaged)**

For those already inside the House. Used to deepen commitment.

Examples:

  - “Continue your journey.”

  - “Step into the next level.”

**4. The Threshold (Activated)**

Used at moments of identity transition. This is your most cinematic CTA.

Examples:

  - “Enter the House.”

  - “Begin Your Return.”

Your VA must choose the correct CTA energy for each page.

**⭐ PART 5 — THE CTA PLACEMENT MAP**

**Where CTAs Go — And Why**

**Carousels**

  - Slide 1: No CTA

  - Slide 2–4: Micro‑invitation

  - Final Slide: Threshold CTA

**Reels / Short‑Form**

  - First 1–2 seconds: No CTA

  - Mid‑video: Soft invitation

  - Final 2 seconds: Directive CTA

**Long‑Form Pages**

  - After the emotional climax

  - After the identity shift

  - End with a soft invitation or threshold CTA

**Website Pages**

  - CTA always at the bottom

  - CTA always after identity clarity

  - CTA always after emotional grounding

  - CTA always after narrative completion

Your VA must follow this map exactly.
